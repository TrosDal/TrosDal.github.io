Biography

I was born in Ponce, Puerto Rico, but moved to Florida at a young age. I graduated from Columbia High School in 2007, and earned a Bachelor's Degree in Anthropology in 2011 from the University of Florida. In 2017 I decided to return to school for a degree in Computer Science, and am currently studying as a Senior at Valdosta State University, where I plan to graduate in the Fall of 2020.


Objective

My goal is to work as a software developer. I approach software development like a puzzle; the solution of which is a fully functional software system that accomplishes the desired goals, and the rules of the puzzle are the constraints on the developmental tools at your disposal, or the ones you are permitted to use. I enjoy the challenge of discovering a way to implement a feature or create an algorithm to properly perform a desired task, using only the tools at hand. For this reason, I desire to develop software as a profession.

Working, Teaching, Research Experience

For the Data Structures course in the Fall of 2018 I was part of a team that developed a working game that emulates Uno, including a fully-featured GUI. This was done to display the ability of Circularly Doubly Linked Lists (CDLL) to allow going back and forth through the turn order of players. We also used the CDLL to emulate a Netflix video browser that allows you to scroll through videos in a circular loop, returning to the beginning of the list after reaching the end, also with a GUI. This paper was published as part of the International Conference of the Foundations of Computer Science in 2019. (Link to the paper here.)
